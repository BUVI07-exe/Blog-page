Rich Dad Poor Dad

Author: Robert Kiyosaki

Published: 1997

Genre: Personal Finance, Business

Pages: 207

Reading Time: Approximately 4 hours

Book Summary

"Rich Dad Poor Dad" by Robert Kiyosaki is a financial game-changer that challenges everything you thought you knew about money. Through the contrasting wisdom of his two "dads"—his educated but financially struggling biological father and his wealthy, entrepreneurial best friend's father—Kiyosaki powerfully illustrates that true wealth comes from acquiring assets, not just earning a paycheck.

It's a wake-up call to prioritize financial literacy, understand the crucial difference between assets (things that put money in your pocket) and liabilities (things that take money out), and ultimately, to make your money work for you, rather than working your entire life just for money.

Key Lessons

  • The rich don't work for money - they make money work for them
  • Understand the difference between assets and liabilities
  • Financial literacy is more important than academic education
  • Take calculated risks and learn from failures
  • Build passive income streams through investments
